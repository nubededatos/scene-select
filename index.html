Top 10 Criterion Collection Films

8½ (1963)

Directed by Federico Fellini, this film is a semi-autobiographical tale that delves into the mind of a film director struggling with creative block. It's celebrated for its innovative narrative structure and visual style.

The 400 Blows (1959)

François Truffaut's debut feature is a cornerstone of the French New Wave, offering a poignant depiction of adolescent rebellion and societal neglect.

The Seven Samurai (1954)

Akira Kurosawa's epic tale of samurai defending a village is a masterclass in storytelling and has influenced countless films worldwide.

Tokyo Story (1953)

Yasujiro Ozu's meditation on family and the passage of time is revered for its emotional depth and minimalist style.

The Battle of Algiers (1966)

Gillo Pontecorvo's gripping portrayal of the Algerian struggle for independence is noted for its documentary-style realism and political impact.

Chungking Express (1994)

Wong Kar-wai's vibrant exploration of love and loneliness in Hong Kong is celebrated for its stylish cinematography and evocative soundtrack.

Au Hasard Balthazar (1966)

Robert Bresson's tale of a donkey's life serves as a profound commentary on human nature and suffering.

Eyes Without a Face (1960)

Georges Franju's haunting film about a surgeon's obsession with restoring his daughter's disfigured face is both eerie and poetic.

Contempt (1963)

Jean-Luc Godard's exploration of marital breakdown set against the backdrop of filmmaking is noted for its visual beauty and thematic complexity.
